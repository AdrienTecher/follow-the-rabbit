The Iceberg

In life, technology and science, there are a lot of different levels of organization and complexity we reason and operate on. For example we can consider this simplistic scale to reason about the human nature:
atoms --> molecules --> cells --> organs --> organisms --> individuals --> societies
Information technology has scales that go from fundamental physics to applications/software. Knowing how things fit together is perhaps more important for a non-specialist than having expertise in a particular domain. That said for navigating the modern world, prioritizing understanding the higher levels of complexities (such as individuals or websites) will give you more operating and communication capabilities. In that regard start by having a basic feel for how you go from electricity to a website or app. Then whenever you are curious or need it, you can go learn more about one specific complexity level.
